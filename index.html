Materials Engineer & Researcher

I’m Nethra Balachandar, a Materials Engineer and Master’s student at Purdue University, specializing in semiconductor process optimization, materials characterization, and yield improvement. My experience spans the semiconductor, battery materials, and e-mobility industries, where I’ve worked in cleanroom environments on projects ranging from DOE-based process optimization to AI-powered wafer defect detection.

With a dual background in Materials Science and Chemical Engineering, I enjoy combining experimental design, statistical process control, and machine learning to translate complex manufacturing data into practical, high-impact solutions. Whether it’s optimizing wafer yields or improving material reliability, I approach challenges with both a detail-oriented and big-picture mindset.

I’m skilled in tools like JMP, Python, MATLAB, and Ansys, and I’m passionate about creating workflows that improve efficiency, reliability, and scalability in manufacturing. I also thrive at the intersection of engineering and data science, where process knowledge meets predictive analytics.

Outside of engineering, I enjoy exploring design tools, experimenting with data visualizations, and planning travel adventures that inspire creativity. From architectural symmetry to natural landscapes, I’m always looking for ways to connect ideas across disciplines and bring fresh perspectives to my work.
